About Me

I am an engineer with a background in computer and electrical engineering, focused on embedded systems, low-level software, and computer architecture. My interests lie at the boundary between hardware and software, where system behavior, performance, and correctness are closely intertwined. I enjoy working with constrained systems and understanding how design decisions at one layer affect behavior across the entire stack.

My experience includes embedded firmware development in C/C++, Bluetooth Low Energy systems, RTOS concepts, and architectural modeling. I’ve worked with microcontrollers, development kits, and hardware debugging tools, and I’m comfortable navigating complex toolchains and technical documentation. I place a strong emphasis on first-principles reasoning and disciplined debugging, preferring to understand root causes rather than relying on trial-and-error fixes.

I approach engineering problems methodically—breaking them down, validating assumptions, and iterating until the system’s behavior is well understood. I value clean design, maintainability, and clear communication, especially in low-level environments where small mistakes can have outsized effects.

This page serves as a space to document my projects, ideas, and ongoing learning as I continue to grow as an engineer working close to the hardware–software interface.
